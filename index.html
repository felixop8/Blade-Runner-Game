How to Play

You are a Replicant

A near-perfect android copy of a human being, built for slave labor. You have escaped from captivity and are attempting to pass as a human. There is only one thing that can give you away: your emotions. Android systems can't perfectly copy human emotional responses. Humans use an empathy response examinaiton, the Voight Kampff test, to identify replicants.

Each round of the test, you are given a question designed to provoke your emotions. You must exhibit the correct emtional response in three categories: Feeling, Tone, and Intensity. If your response is abnormal, the examiner will become suspicious. They will ask you follow up quesitons until they are satisfied with your answer. If you pass enough questions, you'll convince them you are human and you go free. If you fail too many, you'll be violently "retired".

Gameplay

Each Round, the examiner chooses a question and secretly decides the combination of emotions they want to provke. The correct answer consists of three parts: feeling, tone, and intensity. These answers are arranged around a pentagon.

When you answer, the examiner checks how close your response is to the emotion they wanted to provoke. The farther around the pentagon, the more suspicious they become. Example: The examiner has selected a question with a response of (Fear,Distant,Focused). The player answers (Anger,Rude,Focused). Anger is one place from Fear, Rude is two places from Distant, and Focused is correct, so the player gains 3 points of suspicion. If you can intuit the correct response before they get to 20 suspicion, the examiner will believe you are human and you win the round. Win three rounds and you convince them of your humanity and you win the game. Lose three rounds, and you are "retired".

Difficulty

Newer models of replicants have more sensitive emotional systems, so they have an easier time reading the examiner to determine the correct response.

Easy: The Nexus 6 Series of replicant has sensitive emotional detectors and can carefully adjust their responses. When they submit an answer, the category fields will color code to show how clost their answer was to correct. Green for correct answers, Yellow for answers that are off by one point, Red for answers off by two points.

Medium: The Nexus 5 series is less sensitive, so has a harder time interpreting the examiner's intent. They still recieve color coded responses after each question, but the order may be randomized. If they submit one answer that is off by one point, and two answers off by two points, their categories will color yellow, red, and red. The order of these may or may not be correct.

Hard: The Nexus 4 series doesn't have the sensitive emotional methods of the newer models. They can detect the suspicion of the examiner, but can't intrepret the details. They do not recieve any color coding for their responses.
